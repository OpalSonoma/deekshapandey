01 — AI Governance

Pravaah C4I
Governance Dashboard

A research reference implementation for accountable AI in national security command-and-control.

Why I Built This

Built to demonstrate that AI in high-risk, dual-use contexts can be governed with restraint and accountability built in. The central argument is that governance doesn't have to be bolted on after the fact — it can be structural. In contexts where AI systems carry consequential, potentially irreversible weight, the question of how accountability gets designed in is not a compliance question. It's an architecture question. This dashboard is an attempt to answer it.

What It Does

The dashboard models a C4I (Command, Control, Communications, Computers, and Intelligence) governance environment. It includes an AI Restraint Index that scores system behaviour against accountability criteria, fairness auditing, legal bundle generation for audit trails, and a humanitarian oversight layer that flags decisions with civilian impact. The design is deliberately editorial — governance made legible, not buried in logs.

A Note

This is a research reference implementation only. It is not connected to any live system or classified data. It is a demonstration of what accountable AI infrastructure could look like — not what any existing system looks like.

02 — Legal Access

Nyaya-Sāra

Plain-language access to Indian law — BNS, BNSS, and BSA — for people who need to know their rights.

Why I Built This

Built because ordinary people shouldn't need a lawyer to know their rights. India's criminal law underwent a wholesale recodification in 2023 — the Indian Penal Code became the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), the Code of Criminal Procedure became the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS), and the Indian Evidence Act became the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA). The statutes are now new, the awareness is not. Nyaya-Sāra sits in that gap: it translates statute into plain language for anyone who needs to understand what the law says about their situation.

What It Does

Nyaya-Sāra is a conversational tool built on Gemini that lets users ask questions about Indian law in natural language and receive answers grounded in the actual text of BNS, BNSS, and BSA — as well as the Fundamental Rights enshrined in the Constitution of India and landmark judgements by the Supreme Court. It is not a legal advice service — it is a legal literacy tool. The distinction matters: it tells you what the law says, clearly and accessibly.

03 — Learning Design

The Cricket Club

A friendly, approachable cricket learning app with 180+ terms and spaced repetition.

Why I Built This

Built out of curiosity about how learning design works. Cricket has a vocabulary problem: the sport is enormous, the terminology is dense, and most people who love it learned by osmosis. The Cricket Club is an experiment in whether you can build something genuinely fun and well-scaffolded — not a quiz, not a glossary, but a learning environment that respects the intelligence of the person using it. Spaced repetition does a lot of the heavy lifting. The tone does the rest.

What It Does

180+ cricket terms, organised and surfaced through spaced repetition so the things you don't know keep coming back until you do. Designed to be friendly without being condescending — approachable for a complete newcomer, still useful for someone filling in gaps. The aesthetic is warm and low-stakes on purpose.

04 — Professional Simulation

CLOSER

Play the room. Learn the move.

Why I Built This

Built while watching Suits eating a red velvet cupcake. The premise was simple: what if you could practice high-stakes professional conversations — negotiations, difficult feedback, room-reading — with the most demanding coaches imaginable? Harvey Specter has no patience for bad framing. Donna sees through everything. Jessica Pearson sets the standard and doesn't lower it. CLOSER puts you in the room with them and makes you earn your way through.

What It Does

A professional development simulator built as a Claude artifact. You choose your scenario, you make your moves, and you get feedback from Harvey, Mike, Donna, and Jessica — each with a distinct lens and a distinct tolerance for weakness. The bigger vision is a multi-show persona-building platform. For now: Suits. The room is yours. Don't waste it.

About

Deeksha Pandey

Systems thinker. Senior Editor. Building at the intersection of AI and governance.

I'm a systems thinker and Senior Editor at Nielsen with a Master's in International Relations focused on forced migration. For the past year I've been building at the intersection of AI and governance — not as a technologist, but as someone who thinks about systems, accountability, and who gets left out when infrastructure fails.

The pivot isn't a career change so much as a clarification. The same questions that drove my research on displacement — who has power, who bears risk, who gets access — turn out to be exactly the right questions to ask about AI. So I started building tools that demonstrate what that thinking looks like in practice: governance frameworks for high-risk AI deployment, legal access tools for people who can't afford lawyers, learning infrastructure for knowledge that's gatekept by jargon.

The four projects in this portfolio are two sides of the same argument. The C4I Dashboard works top-down: if you're going to deploy AI in consequential contexts, accountability has to be structural, not aspirational. Nyaya-Sāra, The Cricket Club, and CLOSER work bottom-up: access should scale, and the tools that enable it should be genuinely good.

My current focus is AI governance and policy — the question of how the rules get written, and whether they'll hold.
